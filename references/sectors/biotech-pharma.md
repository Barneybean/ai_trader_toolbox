# Biotech & pharma — playbook

Clinical-stage and commercial. A pipeline-and-cash story, not an earnings story.

**What sets the price:**
- The **pipeline** — programs × phase × probability-of-success × addressable market.
- The **cash runway** — months of cash at current burn: can it *reach* the next catalyst
  without dilution?
- The **binary catalyst calendar** — trial readouts, PDUFA/FDA decisions, AdCom votes.

**Catalysts to hunt (+ source):** Phase 2/3 topline readouts, PDUFA dates, FDA/EMA decisions,
AdCom votes, partnership/licensing deals, label expansions (ClinicalTrials.gov, FDA calendars,
company pipeline pages, ASCO/JPM decks). Each is a dated binary — map them and size for gap risk.

**Valuation:** sum-of-parts / **rNPV** of the pipeline + net cash; commercial names on peak-sales
× probability, discounted. **Mispricing signal:** cash ≈ market cap (pipeline priced at ~zero)
with real assets in the clinic.

**Red flags:** <12 months cash before a catalyst (dilution risk); single-asset binary; going-
concern / "Noncompliant" listing status (e.g. a sub-$1 name on a noncompliance notice); wide spreads / micro-float; management with
a serial-dilution history.

**Primary sources:** 10-K/10-Q (cash, burn), ClinicalTrials.gov, FDA calendars, pipeline pages,
conference (ASCO/JPM) presentations.

**Archetype — Moderna at ~$20 (pipeline + cash-floor):** the mentor read — rNPV of the
non-COVID pipeline (oncology/INT, RSV, flu, rare disease), cash + investments **≈ or > market
cap** (pipeline priced at zero), a runway that reaches the catalysts, and upcoming Phase 2/3
readouts as the re-rating triggers → deep-undervalued quality with a cash floor. Ran to $70+.

**Variant angle:** an rNPV the market ignores because it fixates on one asset; a cash floor
under a hated name; a catalyst the generalist calendar misses.
