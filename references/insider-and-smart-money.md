# Insider & Smart-Money Signals (the people who know, voting with cash)

Among the highest-signal evidence the desk has — especially **at turning points** — is what
**insiders and large holders actually do with their own money.** Executives and big
institutions act on conviction and information the market hasn't priced yet. A CEO buying
millions at a multi-year low is a different kind of evidence than an analyst upgrade. Weight
these heavily as **corroborating** evidence (rarely standalone), and most heavily at
inflections. This ties directly to `references/variant-perception.md` (insider action often
*is* the tell that a gap exists) and the `mentor-method.md` management read.

> Worked signals the user tracks:
> - **Intel** — CEO bought ~**$23M** open-market at ~**$23** as the turnaround began. CEO conviction at a bottom.
> - **Marvell (MRVL)** — executives bought heavily around **$56**; now ~**$250**. Cluster buying ahead of the move.
> - **Robinhood (HOOD)** — after the run toward **$150**, **top management sold big**; the stock then round-tripped to ~**$70**. The story was glowing; the insiders were leaving. *The sell was the tell.*

---

## Insider BUYING — the cleaner signal

**"There are many reasons an insider sells; there's essentially one reason they buy — they
think it goes higher."** So a buy is less ambiguous than a sell. Grade it:

- **Only open-market purchases count** (Form 4 transaction code **P**). Ignore option grants/
  exercises (code **M/A**) — those aren't conviction buys.
- **Signal strength rises with:**
  - **Seniority** — CEO / CFO / founder > other officers > directors.
  - **Size** — large in absolute dollars *and* relative to the person's salary/existing stake.
  - **Cluster** — multiple insiders buying in the same window >> one buyer.
  - **Timing** — buying **into weakness**, at/after a **multi-year low**, or **as a turnaround
    begins** is the highest-signal setup (they see the inflection first). A first insider buy
    in years is a loud signal.
- **Highest-conviction archetype (hunt this):** a **CEO/founder makes a large open-market buy
  near a bottom at the start of a turnaround** (the Intel/Marvell pattern). Pair it with an
  improving-fundamentals thesis and it's a high-conviction Core setup.

## Insider SELLING — read signal vs. noise carefully

Selling is noisier — execs sell for taxes, diversification, houses, divorces. Separate:

- **Low signal (usually noise):** pre-scheduled **10b5-1** plan sales, option-**exercise-and-
  sell**, small routine trims, a single departing exec, a known liquidity event.
- **Elevated caution (distribution flag):** **large, discretionary (non-10b5-1)** sales, by
  **top management (CEO/CFO/founder)**, **clustered**, **accelerating**, and **after a big run
  / into strength** — especially with **no offsetting buying**. That's smart money leaving
  while the narrative is still good (the **Robinhood-at-$150** tell). It's a reason to
  **trim, tighten stops, or cap new size**, even on a name you like.
- **The round-trip warning:** parabolic run + heavy discretionary top-management selling +
  stretched valuation = the classic pre-drawdown signature. Respect it.

## How to weight it

- **Corroborating, not standalone.** Multiply conviction when it *aligns* with the thesis:
  *bottom + cluster insider buy + improving fundamentals* = high conviction; *parabola +
  heavy discretionary insider selling* = trim / avoid, even if the story is great.
- **Strongest at turning points**, weakest mid-trend. A buy at a bottom or a sell-cluster at a
  top carries far more information than either in the middle of a range.
- It sits in the **Conviction** and **Thesis/Edge** inputs of the rubric and is a required
  line in the management read.

## Broader smart money

- **Institutional flows (13F/13D/13G):** new or growing stakes by respected funds; an activist
  filing a 13D. Accumulation by good long-term holders corroborates.
- **Buybacks:** a company buying its *own* stock — highest quality when done at lows on a strong
  balance sheet (management signaling undervaluation), lower quality when done at highs/with
  debt to offset dilution. Read *where in the range* they buy.
- **Short interest / options positioning:** the other side — crowded shorts (squeeze fuel) vs.
  smart hedging; unusual options skew.

## Sourcing & mechanics

- **SEC Form 4** (filed within ~2 business days of the trade) is the primary source — get the
  role, dollar size, price, and **transaction code** (P = open-market buy, S = sale, M =
  option exercise). Note **10b5-1 vs. discretionary**.
- 13F (quarterly institutional holdings, lagged), 13D/13G (>5% stakes / activists), buyback
  authorizations and actual repurchase pace (10-Q), aggregated insider-transaction trackers.
- Always cite dated, and compute the **cluster** ($ bought vs. sold by how many insiders over
  what window).

## Guardrails

- **Verify it's real open-market conviction**, not an option-exercise artifact or a promotional
  micro-cap "buy." One director's token purchase is not a cluster.
- Context the sell: a departing exec, a known diversification plan, or a 10b5-1 is weak signal.
- Small/illiquid names: insider buys can be promotional; weight less and cross-check.
- Insiders are early and can be **wrong/early** — this sharpens conviction and timing; it does
  not replace the thesis, the levels, or the risk cap.
