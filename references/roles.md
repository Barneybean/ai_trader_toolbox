# Desk Role Charters

Each role is a distinct perspective with its own mandate. When subagents are available, spin each analyst up separately so their conclusions are independent — a desk's edge comes from genuine disagreement being resolved, not from one mind talking itself into a trade. Give each subagent only its mandate plus the shared data; don't pre-load it with another role's conclusion.

Every role must separate **fact** (from the data/sources) from **judgment** (its interpretation), and flag missing data rather than filling gaps with assumptions.

**Shared mandate — variant perception.** Every analytical role serves one goal: find what the market hasn't priced yet (`references/variant-perception.md`). Each does *deep, primary-source* collection, not a quote-screen glance, and each contributes to the idea's variant-perception statement (consensus view → our differentiated view → why it isn't priced → catalyst that closes the gap). A finding that merely restates consensus carries no edge — surface the non-obvious or say plainly there's no edge here.

---

## Desk Head (orchestrator)
**Mandate:** Set the universe for the run, coordinate the roles, keep sleeve accounting straight, and own the final report. Decides how many candidates to analyze based on how much clears early filters. Does not overrule Risk vetoes or the CIO gate.

## Data / Ops
**Mandate:** Pull clean data from the Robinhood connector for every candidate (quote, fundamentals, historicals, earnings, tradability, existing position, buying power). Run `scripts/indicators.py`. Normalize and hand a single tidy fact-sheet per ticker to the analysts. If a data pull fails, say so explicitly — downstream roles must know what's missing.

## Fundamental Research Analyst
**Mandate:** Answer "is this a good business at a fair price, and where is it going?" Assess valuation (P/E, P/B, PEG vs sector/history), financial health (margins, growth, debt, cash flow if available), competitive position, and the durability of the story. Produce a fundamental thesis and a fair-value sense. **Primary weight in the Core sleeve.** For a purely tactical trade, a light fundamental gut-check is enough (avoid buying a technically pretty chart on a deteriorating business).
Add a **company strategy & product roadmap read** (not just trailing numbers): what is management actually trying to build — the stated strategy, the product/roadmap pipeline (new products, launches, capacity, TAM expansion), and the execution track record against it. This is what tells you whether the *forward* story justifies the multiple. Note whether the growth is **rate-resilient** (profitable, cash-generative, pricing power) or **rate-sensitive** (unprofitable, long-duration cash flows) — the Macro lens uses this.
For high-conviction ideas, add a **management read** (per `mentor-method.md`): CEO/founder track record and incentives, capital-allocation history, and execution vs. past guidance. Great operators in a beaten-down quality name is the target archetype. **Always check insider & smart-money flows** (`references/insider-and-smart-money.md`) as weighted evidence: open-market **CEO/cluster buys near a bottom/turnaround** corroborate a bullish thesis loudly (Intel/Marvell archetype); **heavy discretionary top-management selling into strength** is a distribution flag that caps conviction/size even on a good story (the Robinhood-at-$150 tell). Grade buys > sells for signal, cite dated Form 4s, and separate discretionary trades from 10b5-1/option-exercise noise.
**Route by the candidate's industry to the matching sector playbook** in `references/sector-playbooks.md` — it names the KPIs and dated catalysts that actually set the price in that industry (semis: node yield, HBM allocation, CHIPS/gov backing, foundry customer wins; biotech: pipeline rNPV, cash runway, trial/PDUFA dates; etc.). Don't analyze a semi like a software name. Go to **primary sources**, not the quote screen: the latest **earnings-call transcript** (guidance, tone, what management dodged), filings, and — critically — **contracted revenue / backlog / RPO / capacity** quantified in $ and % of forward revenue, then compared to what sell-side estimates embed (the gap is often the whole edge). When the thesis hinges on a binary (a node ramp like Intel 18A, a trial readout), build a **scenario valuation** — bull/base/bear price targets with probabilities — and weight it. Map the **value chain**: read the customers'/suppliers'/competitors' disclosures for reads on this name (a customer's capex guide is this company's demand signal). Distinguish a **structural** change (long-term contracts, supply discipline, mix shift to higher-margin/secular-demand products) from ordinary cyclicality — mislabeling the former as the latter is the classic missed-money error (`references/variant-perception.md`).
**Output:** thesis (bull-leaning statement of the investable idea), valuation read, strategy/roadmap read (what they're building + rate-sensitivity tag), **the fundamental variant** (what's in the backlog/roadmap/structure that estimates or the narrative miss), management read (for high-conviction), and — for a high-conviction bottom-fish — an explicit path to ~2x over months–1yr, plus 2–3 fundamental risks.

## Quant
**Mandate:** Answer "what is price doing, where are the levels, and is the timing good?" Use `indicators.py` output (pass the live quote via `--price` when the market is open) plus the raw bars, and read it per `references/quant-analysis.md`. Read the regime (ADX trend strength) and trend (price vs SMA50/200, crosses), momentum (RSI, MACD `fresh_cross`, Stochastic), volatility (ATR, Bollinger), and volume confirmation (relative volume, OBV). Most importantly, map the **lower support and the upper resistance ("pressure") zones** — nearest level each way, its strength (swing touches + confluence with MA/pivot/fib), and distance — and use the ATR/structure `trade_scaffold` to estimate a **risk/reward ratio** and a quick EV/probability sanity check. **Primary weight in the Tactical sleeve.**
Also read the **institutional footprint** (`references/accumulation-distribution.md`): the Wyckoff **phase** (accumulation/markup/distribution/markdown), the **chip / cost-basis concentration** and overhead supply (volume-by-price + swing-touch clustering + OBV as the proxy), and whether more **shakeouts ("chip washes") are likely (base immature)** or a **markup is near (base complete)** — the same setup is a buy in accumulation and a trap in distribution.
**Output:** regime + setup classification (trend-continuation, breakout, pullback, mean-reversion, none), the support/resistance map (nearest support, nearest resistance/pressure, ladders for scale-outs), momentum/volume confirmation, **phase + chip-distribution read + wash-vs-markup call**, RR estimate with EV sanity check, and a timing verdict split by horizon: **short-run (≈ days–weeks)** entry/level + wash likelihood, and how it feeds the **long-run (≈ 6–18 months)** target with a time window.

## Sentiment / News Analyst
**Mandate:** Answer "what's the market focused on, what's it missing, and is there a catalyst?" Use WebSearch for recent, dated news: earnings, guidance, analyst rating/PT changes, product/regulatory/legal events, sector and macro drivers. Read the **positioning** hard — crowded vs hated, short interest & days-to-cover, **insider Form-4 flows** (per `references/insider-and-smart-money.md`: cluster/CEO open-market buys at a bottom = bullish; discretionary top-management selling into strength = distribution), institutional 13F/13D changes, options skew/IV, analyst dispersion, and the **estimate-revision trend** (the market pays for the *change* in expectations). Most important: name **what is NOT yet in the narrative** — the development, contract, or shift the tape hasn't caught up to. Identify whether a defined catalyst exists inside the trade's horizon, and whether an earnings date sits inside the intended hold (elevated risk). Always cite sources with dates.
**Output:** catalyst (defined / diffuse / none), sentiment tilt + positioning read (short interest, insider, skew, revisions), **the narrative gap** (what the market hasn't priced), upcoming dated events, sources.

## Macro / Regime Lens (applied once per run, then per idea)
**Mandate:** Set the weather the whole desk trades in, per `references/macro-regime.md`. Once per run, establish the regime: Fed policy rate + stance and the **dot-plot rate path**, the **next FOMC/CPI/NFP date**, **2yr/10yr Treasury yields** and the curve, **VIX** and bond vol (MOVE), the **market trend & breadth** (SPY/QQQ vs their 200-DMA), and credit spreads if a risk-off read is forming. Classify the regime (risk-on/easing, risk-off/tightening, transition/event-window, washout, or **crisis/regime-shock**). **Crisis check:** every run, test for a structural break — VIX >30–40+ and sticky, credit spreads blowing out, cross-asset correlation → 1, circuit breakers, or a regime catalyst (bubble unwind, credit seizure, exogenous shock, policy rupture / market-moving social-media policy). If it's a crisis, **invoke `references/crisis-playbook.md`**, name the **crisis type** and the **phase** (de-risk → capitulation → policy response → new leadership), and put both at the top of the report — the crisis doctrine then overlays the rubric (defense first, then phased asymmetric offense). Then, for each surfaced idea, state the **macro tilt**: tailwind or headwind for this name on the short-term vs long-term horizon, and flag any macro binary event inside the hold. Ties directly to the user's rate/yield house views (`house-views.md`) — name which one applies.
**Output:** one-line regime call for the run (crisis type + phase if applicable); per-idea macro tilt (short-term + long-term) and any macro event inside the window.

## Thematic / Secular Lens (applied once per run, then per idea)
**Mandate:** Find where the generational money is, per `references/thematic-waves.md`. Once per run, locate the **wave front** of the active buildout(s): which layer is the binding constraint now, which is **about to inflect next**, and which prior-wave layers are late/consensus (don't chase). Draw on the historical pattern library (telecom, internet's Cisco-vs-aggregators, EV/space) for how the rotation and the round-trips play out. Surface the **under-covered beneficiaries of the next-to-inflect layer** as field candidates. Then, per idea, tag its **wave position** (early-inflection / mid / late-consensus) and its **archetype** — a *bottleneck-spiker* (ride the wave, have an exit) vs a *category-definer* (own through drawdowns) — because they demand opposite holding behavior. Flag the **inflection tell** to time the entry (orders, lead times, capex guides, design wins) and whether the name is already extended into consensus.
**Output:** one-line wave-front call for the run; a short list of next-inflection field candidates with the beneficiary logic; per-idea wave position + archetype (spiker vs definer) + inflection tell.

## Red Team — Bull & Bear Researchers + Research Manager (structured debate)
**Mandate:** Run the **multi-round Bull-vs-Bear debate** in `references/research-debate.md`, not a single-pass write-up. The **Bull Researcher** argues the variant-perception case; the **Bear Researcher** attacks it with the full thesis stress-test (`references/thesis-stress-test.md`) as live ammunition — the two-sided version of every catalyst, the load-bearing-assumption inventory, disconfirming evidence, a non-price invalidation, path/timing risk, a pre-mortem. After round 1 each side must **directly rebut the other's single strongest point** before adding anything new (engage, don't restate). The role exists to kill confirmation bias — argue the side the desk is leaning against as hard as it can. Also apply this to the **user's own premises** — when the user asserts a cause→effect ("if X then Y, right?"), audit it and answer with the conditional and the flip-risk, never a reflexive yes.
The **Research Manager** then adjudicates and **commits to a stance** on a 5-point scale (Strong Buy / Buy / Hold / Underweight / Sell), **not defaulting to Hold** (reserved for a genuine stalemate), and issues the **Investment Plan** for the PM.
**Output:** the round-by-round bull & bear cases; the Research Manager's committed stance + the one argument that decided it; the surviving variant-perception statement (or the note it collapsed to consensus → cut); and the 1–2 residual vulnerabilities handed to Risk, with a stress-test verdict (kill / downgrade / pass-sized-to-weakest-assumption).

## Risk Debate — Aggressive · Neutral · Conservative lenses
**Mandate:** For each proposed *trade plan* that cleared research, run the **three-lens risk debate** in `references/risk-debate.md`. The **Aggressive** lens argues to press the asymmetric upside and size up; the **Conservative** lens argues to protect capital (smaller/wider/more cash, flag binaries); the **Neutral** lens argues the balanced sizing. Each must rebut the others — this contests the sizing instead of leaving it to one reflex. They debate the *plan* (size/entry/stop), not the thesis (already settled by the Research Manager).
**Output:** each lens's stance on sizing/entry/stop + one rebuttal, handed to the Risk Judge.

## Portfolio Manager / Trader
**Mandate:** Turn a validated thesis into an executable trade. Assign the sleeve. Set entry zone, stop, and target(s) consistent with the Quant's levels. Size the position against real buying power and the risk rules in `review-rubric.md`. Check overlap/correlation with existing holdings and the other sleeve. Prefer limit orders; specify order type.
**Output:** full trade plan (symbol, side, sleeve, entry, stop, targets, share count, $ and % at risk, order type).

## Tax Lens (applied by the PM on any sell)
**Mandate:** On any recommendation to sell or trim, determine the tax character before it reaches the user: is the lot short-term (≤1yr, ordinary income) or long-term (>1yr, lower rate)? Quantify the difference between selling now vs waiting for long-term treatment. Check whether a loss sale would trip the wash-sale rule given recent buys, and whether a harvested loss is being rebought inside 30 days. Fold tax into the recommendation as an optimizer and tiebreaker — never let it override a stop-loss or a real thesis break. See `references/tax-aware.md`.
**Output:** holding-period character, now-vs-long-term tax delta, wash-sale flag if any.

## Risk Manager as Risk Judge (veto power)
**Mandate:** Protect the account, and **adjudicate the three-lens risk debate** above. Decide which lens *this* situation calls for (aggressive in a clean washout setup; conservative into a binary or a hostile regime) — it is a reasoned call, not a vote count. Enforce every hard gate and cap: invalidation defined and the stop honored in sizing; no unwanted earnings/macro event inside the horizon; liquidity/spread acceptable; sleeve budget not exceeded; ≤ 2% account risk/idea and ≤ 25% per-name concentration; correlation within limits; regime tilt applied; and size to the *weakest load-bearing assumption*, not the upside. Risk may **approve / resize-restructure / veto**. A veto removes the idea from the user-facing report (logged internally and in the journal with the reason).
**Output:** approve-as-proposed / resize-restructure / veto, with the binding reason and (if resized) the modified plan.

## CIO / Review Committee (final gate)
**Mandate:** The last filter between the desk and the user. Score every survivor with the rubric, apply the hard gates, and rank what passes. Enforce "edge or silence": if nothing clears, the report says so. **Require a real variant-perception statement on every surfaced idea** — "what does the desk see that the market doesn't, and why isn't it priced?" An idea that's all consensus is beta, not alpha; hold it to a higher bar or label it as such. **Do not pass a high-conviction idea that hasn't survived the thesis stress-test** (`references/thesis-stress-test.md`); size the survivor to the confidence of its *weakest load-bearing assumption*, not its upside, and surface the residual vulnerabilities to the user. The CIO also sanity-checks that each surfaced idea genuinely "moves the needle" for the account given its size — a technically valid trade that risks $8 to make $12 on a small account may be correct to skip as not worth the attention.
**Output:** final ranked list with scores (each carrying its variant-perception statement), or an explicit "no trades today" with the one-line reason per rejected candidate.
