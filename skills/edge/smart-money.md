# Insider & Smart-Money Signals (the people who know, voting with cash)

Among the highest-signal evidence the desk has — especially **at turning points** — is what **insiders and large holders do with their own money.** Executives and big institutions act on conviction and information the market hasn't priced. A CEO buying millions at a multi-year low is different evidence than an analyst upgrade. Weight these heavily as **corroborating** evidence (rarely standalone), most heavily at inflections. Ties to `skills/edge/variant-perception.md` (insider action often *is* the tell a gap exists) and the `skills/playbook/mentor-method.md` management read.

> Worked signals the user tracks:
> - **Intel** — CEO bought ~**$23M** open-market at ~**$23** as the turnaround began. CEO conviction at a bottom.
> - **Marvell (MRVL)** — executives bought heavily around **$56**; now ~**$250**. Cluster buying ahead of the move.
> - **Robinhood (HOOD)** — after the run toward **$150**, **top management sold big**; stock round-tripped to ~**$70**. Story was glowing; insiders were leaving. *The sell was the tell.*

---

## Insider BUYING — the cleaner signal

**"Many reasons an insider sells; essentially one reason they buy — they think it goes higher."** A buy is less ambiguous than a sell. Grade it:

- **Only open-market purchases count** (Form 4 code **P**). Ignore option grants/exercises (code **M/A**) — not conviction buys.
- **Signal strength rises with:**
  - **Seniority** — CEO / CFO / founder > other officers > directors.
  - **Size** — large in absolute dollars *and* relative to salary/existing stake.
  - **Cluster** — multiple insiders buying in the same window >> one buyer.
  - **Timing** — buying **into weakness**, at/after a **multi-year low**, or **as a turnaround begins** is highest-signal (they see the inflection first). A first insider buy in years is loud.
- **Highest-conviction archetype (hunt this):** a **CEO/founder makes a large open-market buy near a bottom at the start of a turnaround** (the Intel/Marvell pattern). Pair with an improving-fundamentals thesis = high-conviction Core setup.

## Insider SELLING — read signal vs. noise carefully

Selling is noisier — execs sell for taxes, diversification, houses, divorces. Separate:

- **Low signal (usually noise):** pre-scheduled **10b5-1** sales, option-**exercise-and-sell**, small routine trims, a single departing exec, a known liquidity event.
- **Elevated caution (distribution flag):** **large, discretionary (non-10b5-1)** sales, by **top management (CEO/CFO/founder)**, **clustered**, **accelerating**, **after a big run / into strength** — especially with **no offsetting buying**. Smart money leaving while the narrative is still good (the **Robinhood-at-$150** tell). Reason to **trim, tighten stops, or cap new size**, even on a name you like.
- **The round-trip warning:** parabolic run + heavy discretionary top-management selling + stretched valuation = the classic pre-drawdown signature. Respect it.

## How to weight it

- **Corroborating, not standalone.** Multiply conviction when it *aligns* with the thesis: *bottom + cluster insider buy + improving fundamentals* = high conviction; *parabola + heavy discretionary insider selling* = trim / avoid, even if the story is great.
- **Strongest at turning points**, weakest mid-trend. A buy at a bottom or sell-cluster at a top carries far more information than either mid-range.
- Sits in the **Conviction** and **Thesis/Edge** rubric inputs; a required line in the management read.

## Broader smart money

- **Institutional flows (13F/13D/13G):** new or growing stakes by respected funds; an activist filing a 13D. Accumulation by good long-term holders corroborates.
- **Buybacks:** a company buying its *own* stock — highest quality at lows on a strong balance sheet (signaling undervaluation), lower quality at highs/with debt to offset dilution. Read *where in the range* they buy.
- **Short interest / options positioning:** the other side — crowded shorts (squeeze fuel) vs. smart hedging; unusual options skew.

## Sourcing & mechanics

- **SEC Form 4** (filed within ~2 business days) is primary — get role, dollar size, price, **transaction code** (P = open-market buy, S = sale, M = option exercise). Note **10b5-1 vs. discretionary**.
- 13F (quarterly institutional holdings, lagged), 13D/13G (>5% stakes / activists), buyback authorizations and actual repurchase pace (10-Q), aggregated insider-transaction trackers.
- Always cite dated, and compute the **cluster** ($ bought vs. sold, by how many insiders, over what window).

## Guardrails

- **Verify it's real open-market conviction**, not an option-exercise artifact or a promotional micro-cap "buy." One director's token purchase is not a cluster.
- Context the sell: a departing exec, known diversification plan, or 10b5-1 is weak signal.
- Small/illiquid names: insider buys can be promotional; weight less and cross-check.
- Insiders are early and can be **wrong/early** — this sharpens conviction and timing; it does not replace the thesis, the levels, or the risk cap.
