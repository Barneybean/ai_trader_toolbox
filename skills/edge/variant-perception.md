# Variant Perception & Deep Research (the desk's real edge)

**Premise:** you make money only when your view is *differentiated AND correct*. Consensus is in the price — restating it (P/E, the chart, "great company," "cyclical, avoid") has zero edge. The money is in **what the crowd hasn't priced**: the structural change mislabeled as noise, the backlog not in estimates, the second-order beneficiary nobody connected. This is the job, not optional depth.

> Cautionary tale: the desk once screened SNDK/MU, saw high P/E and a parabola, called "cyclical value trap — avoid." The real story was **$100B+ of contract-locked multi-year revenue** (MU's 2026 HBM sold out at fixed prices; SanDisk's ~$42B contracted) de-risking the cycle, plus **HBF** as priced-at-zero AI-inference optionality. A quote screen sees the trap; deep research sees the re-rating. Never ship the quote-screen answer.

---

## The variant-perception statement (REQUIRED for every surfaced idea)

No idea reaches the user without this filled in with evidence. If you can't complete it, you have a consensus position, not an edge (which must clear a higher bar — see rubric).

1. **Consensus view:** what does the market believe and price in? (Sell-side/retail narrative, implied expectation.)
2. **Our variant view:** what do we believe that's *different*, specifically? (A number, catalyst, structural read — not a vibe.)
3. **Why we might be right:** the evidence — primary source, quantified.
4. **Why it isn't priced yet:** why the gap exists (under-covered, mislabeled, too-early, too-complex, forced-seller, narrative-lag).
5. **What closes the gap (and when):** the catalyst/timeline forcing a re-rate to our view.
6. **How we're wrong:** disconfirming evidence to watch, and the invalidation.

If steps 1 and 2 are identical, there's no trade — it's beta, not alpha.

## Edge taxonomy — where under-the-radar money hides

Hunt these deliberately. Most winners are one of these before they're obvious:

1. **Secular shift mislabeled as cyclical.** A "commodity cyclical" whose demand/pricing structurally changed (long-term contracts, supply discipline, AI demand). Market keeps applying trough multiples to a de-risked stream. *(The MU/SNDK lesson.)*
2. **Contract / backlog / RPO visibility not in estimates.** Signed multi-year revenue, take-or-pay guarantees, sold-out capacity — quantify in $ and % of forward revenue, compare to what consensus embeds. The gap is the edge.
3. **New product cycle / TAM expansion priced at zero.** Optionality the market ignores because it's pre-revenue (HBF, a new platform, a regulatory approval). Value as a call option: probability × payoff, and what de-risks it.
4. **Value-chain / second-order beneficiaries.** If X is booming, who supplies X — and who supplies *them*? The bottleneck, sole-source component, picks-and-shovels name, the customer's customer. Read the leader's call for reads on the under-covered supplier.
5. **Orphaned / under-followed equities.** Spin-offs, post-bankruptcy, recent IPOs, sub-$5B caps with thin coverage, index-exclusion forced selling, complex "hairy" situations retail avoids. Inefficiency lives where few look.
6. **Positioning & sentiment extremes.** Crowded longs to fade; hated names with quietly improving fundamentals. Read short interest, days-to-cover, institutional ownership changes, insider cluster buying, options skew/IV. Extreme positioning is a coiled spring.
7. **Estimate-revision dynamics.** The market pays for the *change* in expectations. Where are numbers revised up/down, is the buy-side ahead of sell-side? Positive revision + still-cheap = the sweet spot.
8. **Capacity / supply–demand inflections.** Utilization, lead times, inventory cycles, pricing turns. When lead times stretch or inventory clears, the pricing inflection precedes the earnings beat.
9. **Policy / regulatory / geopolitical catalysts.** Subsidies (CHIPS, IRA), tariffs, export controls, reshoring, approvals. These reprice whole cohorts before fundamentals show it.
10. **Capital-allocation inflections.** Margin turn, FCF inflection, debt paydown, buyback initiation, activist involvement, insider buying at scale. The narrative follows the cash.

## Deep information-collection playbook (past the quote screen)

The connector gives price/fundamentals — table stakes, not research. For any idea worth size, collect from **primary and second-order sources** (WebSearch; cite dated):

- **Primary company disclosure:** latest **earnings-call transcript** (guidance, backlog/RPO, capacity, pricing, tone, what they dodged), **10-K/10-Q/8-K**, **investor-day decks**, **guidance history vs. delivery** (sandbag or overpromise?). Quantify contracts, backlog, capacity, unit economics.
- **Industry / structural:** standards bodies (JEDEC, OCP), trade press, capacity & pricing trackers, conference commentary, expert/teardown notes. Where "secular vs cyclical" gets settled.
- **Value-chain triangulation:** read **customers', suppliers', competitors'** calls for reads on your name — a hyperscaler's capex guide *is* a memory maker's demand signal. Name the specific link.
- **Positioning & flows:** short interest & days-to-cover, institutional 13F changes, **insider transactions** (cluster buys > one-off sells), options skew/IV-rank, analyst dispersion and recent PT/rating revisions.
- **Alternative / proxy data** where available (web traffic, app ranks, hiring, shipping/booking proxies) — flagged as proxy, not gospel.

Rules: **quantify everything**, **date every source**, **separate fact from inference**, **state confidence** and what would raise it. Missing data is flagged, never guessed.

## Anti-shallowness guardrails

- **The quote-screen test.** If your thesis could come from a single quote + fundamentals screen, it has no edge. Go deeper or don't surface it.
- **Banned as standalone conclusions** (consensus placeholders): "great company, buy the dip," "too expensive, skip," "cyclical, avoid," "it's the leader," "AI tailwind." Allowed only *attached to* a specific, evidenced variant view.
- **Steelman the crowd before fading it.** You can only have an edge against a view you fully understand.
- **Absence of an edge is a finding.** "No variant perception here — fairly priced beta" is a legitimate output. Say it and move on; don't manufacture a story.

## Calibration (don't boil the ocean)

Scale depth to stakes. A quick tactical swing needs levels + one catalyst check. A **Core / high-conviction / larger-size** idea demands the full variant statement + primary-source dossier. Screening the whole universe: run this cheaply as a filter first (which names even *have* a plausible variant angle?), then go deep on the survivors.
