# Biotech & pharma — playbook

Clinical-stage and commercial. A pipeline-and-cash story, not an earnings story.

**What sets the price:**
- **Pipeline** — programs × phase × probability-of-success × addressable market.
- **Cash runway** — months of cash at current burn; does it reach the next catalyst without dilution?
- **Binary catalyst calendar** — trial readouts, PDUFA/FDA decisions, AdCom votes.

**Catalysts to hunt (+ source):** Phase 2/3 topline readouts, PDUFA dates, FDA/EMA decisions,
AdCom votes, partnership/licensing deals, label expansions (ClinicalTrials.gov, FDA calendars,
company pipeline pages, ASCO/JPM decks). Each is a dated binary — map them, size for gap risk.

**Valuation:** sum-of-parts / **rNPV** of pipeline + net cash; commercial names on peak-sales ×
probability, discounted. **Mispricing signal:** cash ≈ market cap (pipeline priced at ~zero) with
real assets in the clinic.

**Red flags:** <12 months cash before a catalyst (dilution); single-asset binary; going-concern /
"Noncompliant" listing status (e.g. sub-$1 name on a noncompliance notice); wide spreads /
micro-float; serial-dilution management.

**Primary sources:** 10-K/10-Q (cash, burn), ClinicalTrials.gov, FDA calendars, pipeline pages,
ASCO/JPM presentations.

**Archetype — Moderna at ~$20 (pipeline + cash-floor):** rNPV of the non-COVID pipeline
(oncology/INT, RSV, flu, rare disease), cash + investments **≈ or > market cap** (pipeline priced
at zero), runway reaching the catalysts, upcoming Phase 2/3 readouts as re-rating triggers →
deep-undervalued quality with a cash floor. Ran to $70+.

**Variant angle:** an rNPV ignored because the market fixates on one asset; a cash floor under a
hated name; a catalyst the generalist calendar misses.
