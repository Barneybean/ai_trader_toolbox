# Semiconductors & hardware — playbook

Covers memory, logic, foundry, fabless/design, equipment — each prices differently.

**What sets the price:**
- **Cyclicals / memory** (MU, SNDK): pricing cycle (DRAM/NAND/HBM ASPs), utilization,
  inventory/lead times, and increasingly **contract-locked revenue/backlog** that de-risks the cycle.
- **Logic / foundry** (INTC, TSM): process-node roadmap & **yield**, capex intensity, customer
  concentration, design wins.
- **Fabless / design** (NVDA, AMD): design wins, TAM, share vs. competitor roadmaps.

**Catalysts to hunt (+ source):** node ramp & yield milestones (earnings call, IEDM papers,
supply-chain checks); **HBM/AI-memory** allocation — sold-out capacity, locked pricing (calls,
pricing trackers); long-term supply agreements (quantify $ and % of forward rev); customer capex
guides (hyperscaler capex = demand); export-control / CHIPS / government-stake news (gov filings);
inventory-correction bottoms (pricing inflection precedes the earnings beat).

**Valuation:** cyclicals on **P/B and mid-cycle (normalized) EPS** — beware low P/E on *peak*
earnings (the trap) and high P/E on *trough* earnings (the opportunity). Logic/foundry on
**scenario DCF** around the node bet. Fabless/design on EV/S vs. growth.

**Red flags:** peak-cycle margins extrapolated forever; single-customer concentration; a node
slip; China/export exposure; inventory build.

**Primary sources:** earnings-call transcripts (capacity, pricing, bookings), JEDEC/OCP standards,
industry pricing trackers, customer capex guides, CHIPS/gov-stake filings.

**Archetype — Intel (process/catalyst story):** not "is INTC cheap" but *is 18A working*
(yield/defect trend, in HVM, ramping products), *how big if it works* (margin recovery + a real
external foundry business → re-rate from "broken IDM" to "US foundry #2"), *how likely* (external
design wins, yield-claim vs. delivery history — handicap it), the *TSMC N2/A16 gap*, *CHIPS & any
US-government equity stake*, *anchor foundry customers*. → Build a **bull/base/bear scenario
valuation** with probabilities; the weighted target vs. price is the edge. (See also the CEO's
$23M open-market buy at ~$23 — `skills/edge/smart-money.md`.)

**Variant angle:** node-yield reality vs. management's public claims; contract/backlog visibility
vs. what estimates embed; who the *next* bottleneck supplier is (`skills/edge/thematic-waves.md`).
