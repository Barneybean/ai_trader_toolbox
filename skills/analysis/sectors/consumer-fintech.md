# Consumer fintech / product-led platforms — playbook

The compounder read: a user-centric, fast-iterating team compounding users and revenue.

**What sets the price:** not this quarter's EPS — the **product-led growth flywheel**: product
velocity (shipping *good* features fast), UX, **stickiness/engagement** (retention, DAU/MAU, ARPU
trend), the **ecosystem/network effect** (each new product raises switching costs), TAM
expansion into adjacencies.

**Catalysts to hunt (+ source):** ecosystem-expanding launches, user/deposit/AUC growth
inflections, monetization (ARPU) steps, geographic expansion, regulatory changes (moat or threat)
(shareholder letters/transcripts, app-store ranks/engagement proxies, Form 4s).

**Valuation:** growth-adjusted (EV/S, EV/gross-profit, P/E once profitable), flywheel as the
durability case — a bet on continued user + ARPU compounding. Watch saturation/decelerating
engagement.

**Red flags:** slowing user/engagement growth; monetization outrunning trust (user backlash);
regulatory hits; **heavy discretionary insider selling into strength** (distribution tell);
rate-sensitive if unprofitable.

**Primary sources:** transcripts + shareholder letters (roadmap, user/ARPU metrics, tone),
app-store/engagement proxies, Form 4 insider activity, regulatory filings.

**Archetype — Robinhood at ~$30:** buyable read was *qualitative* — fast iteration on good UX,
rapid feature rollout, an **ecosystem** push (banking, credit, retirement), a **regulatory/trust
moat** ($2M FDIC sweep insurance right as users feared SVB-style risk), high stickiness,
fast-growing revenue, global expansion → a compounder the market underrated. Ran to ~$150. **Then
the caution:** round-tripped to ~$70 — the tell was **heavy top-management insider selling into
the run** (`skills/edge/smart-money.md`). *Own the compounder early; respect the distribution
signal near the top.*

**Variant angle:** a product/ecosystem flywheel the market values as a brokerage; a trust/
regulatory moat that shows up in deposits before revenue.
